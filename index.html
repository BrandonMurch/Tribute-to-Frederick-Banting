Sir Frederick Banting

Co-Discoverer of Insulin, Nobel Prize Winner

The Life of Frederick Banting.

  • 1891: Frederick Grant Banting was born at Alliston, Ontario, Canada.
  • 1916: Took his M.B. degree at the University of Toronto.
  • 1918: Joined the Canadian Army Medical Corps and served in World War one in France.
  • Later in 1918: Wounded in the line of duty at the battle of Cambrai
  • 1919: Awarded the Military Cross for heroism under fire.
  • later in 1919: Returned to Canada, taking up occupation as general practitioner, resident surgeon for sick children and teaching in orthopaedics.
  • 1921: Along with Dr. Charles Best, Banting discovered insulin.
  • 1922: Awarded his M.D. degree, along with a gold medal.
  • Later in 1922: Became Senior Demonstrator in Medicine at the University of Toronto.
  • 1923: Awarded the Nobel Prize for Physiology and Medicine. He was also elected to the new Banting and Best chair for Medical Research.
  • 1924: Married Marion Robertson, with whom he had his only child, William. He later divorced Marion.
  • 1937: Married his second wife, Henrietta Ball.
  • 1938: Began researching the effects that gravity has on aircraft pilots, the result of which was the invention of the "g-suit".
  • 1941: While flying to England to display a prototype of a new flying suit, one of the engines of the airplane failed. The plane crashed over Labrador. Frederick died of his wounds the following day.
